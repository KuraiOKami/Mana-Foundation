Who we are

Mana means “strength for the journey.”

Founded in Orlando after the 2012 hurricane season, Mana Foundation unites neighbors, partners, and public agencies so every Florida family has a trusted ally when crises hit.

Discover our mission Meet the movement

Our story

Mana Foundation began as a grassroots meal distribution effort organized by friends and neighbors in Orlando. As storms intensified and housing costs soared, we formalized as a nonprofit to provide sustained relief and long-term recovery for families across Central Florida. Today, we coordinate statewide coalitions, deploy mobile pantry units, and invest in policy advocacy to make Florida safer and more equitable for everyone.

2012 — Neighbors helping neighbors

Community cooks hosted block parties to feed families displaced after Hurricane Isaac.

2015 — First regional warehouse

A retired citrus packing facility became our Orlando hub for food, hygiene, and rebuilding supplies.

2018 — Expansion to the Panhandle

Following Hurricane Michael, Mana trained local leaders and opened two rapid-response hubs in the region.

2023 — Policy & preparedness

We launched the Florida Resilience Table, bringing nonprofits and agencies together to influence preparedness funding and equitable recovery.

What guides us

Our values

  • Neighbors first
  • Rapid + relational
  • Joyful service
  • Shared leadership
  • Accountability
  • Equity

Love of neighbor is our organizing principle. We listen deeply before designing any solution, and we measure success by the dignity and agency families feel as they rebuild.

People-powered impact

18 full-time team members, 150+ trained volunteers, and a statewide network of 70 partner organizations keep Mana Foundation nimble yet deeply rooted. Local advisory circles surface needs from Miami-Dade, the Panhandle, the Treasure Coast, and beyond.

We invest in bilingual leadership development, equitable pay, and trauma-informed training to ensure care givers receive the same support they offer others.
