What we do

Three program tracks keep Florida families moving forward.

Immediate relief, community-rooted nourishment, and systems-level advocacy operate in tandem so support reaches every generation.

Community Pantry Network Emergency Relief

Program 01

Community Pantry Network

Our mobile and pop-up pantries operate in food deserts across 37 counties. Rather than one-size-fits-all distributions, each site is co-led by local partners to ensure culturally-relevant staples, fresh produce, and wraparound referrals are available.

Services

  • Weekly neighborhood markets with fresh produce
  • Senior home delivery routes powered by volunteers
  • Bilingual nutrition & cooking classes
  • Benefits navigation for SNAP, WIC, and Medicaid

Infrastructure

Refrigerated box trucks, solar-powered cold storage, and a statewide purchasing co-op keep costs low while prioritizing Florida farmers whenever possible.

Fresh food boxes funded 72% complete

$360K raised toward the $500K goal for 2024 pantry deliveries.

Program 02

Emergency Relief & Disaster Prep

Mana Foundation operates rapid-response hubs from Miami to Pensacola. We stock hurricane kits year-round, deploy mutual aid teams during wildfires or floods, and stay with families as they rebuild—a crucial step often missing in disaster response.

Before a storm

  • Preparedness workshops & household plans
  • Distribution of generators, radios, and chargers
  • Evacuation transportation coordination

During & after

  • Hot meal stations and hydration points
  • Emergency cash grants & temporary housing support
  • Construction volunteer teams and supply drops
Disaster kits pre-staged 58% ready

2,900 kits assembled out of the 5,000-kit hurricane season goal.

Program 03

Education & Advocacy

Relief alone cannot close the gap. Through policy research, leadership development, and civic engagement, Mana Foundation elevates the lived experiences of families navigating hunger, housing instability, and climate disasters.

Key initiatives

  • Florida Resilience Table policy working groups
  • Youth leadership fellowships each summer
  • Listening sessions hosted statewide
  • Annual advocacy day at the Florida Capitol

Policy priorities

  • Affordable housing protections
  • Expanded emergency SNAP/Liheap funding
  • Investment in resilient community infrastructure
  • Language access in emergency communications
Leadership fellows sponsored 40 of 60 slots

Funding secured for 40 youth fellows; seeking support for the remaining 20.

Program 04

Mana Communities

Transitional micro-communities of 8–12 cottages provide dignified housing for neighbors exiting homelessness. Residents receive wraparound case management, apprenticeships in trades, and savings-matching plans to launch into permanent housing within 12 months.

Blueprint

  • Net-zero cottages with secure storage + shared kitchens
  • On-site workforce lab teaching solar, carpentry, culinary, and tech
  • Resident council to co-create neighborhood norms and programming

Immediate needs

  • Land prep + utility hookups in Orange, Hillsborough, Escambia counties
  • Apprenticeship stipends and tool libraries
  • Volunteer crews for cottage finishing
Phase 1 build fund 41% funded

$1.23M committed of the $3M needed for the first 24 cottages.
